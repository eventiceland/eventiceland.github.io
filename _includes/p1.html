Each day, participants will be given a list of objectives or missions for each location, specially tailored to their personal levels of experience. This will allow individuals to branch off and exorcise their creativity on their own, while still actively engaging in group activities. I will also be giving an in-depth and comprehensive discussion about the history of each location, its significance to Icelandic culture, as well as folklore and myths surrounding the specific area; it is my hope that each individual leaves my seminar with not only a bigger understanding of photography and cinematography, but of Iceland and Icelandic culture and music, as a whole.

Day One

  • Pick guests up from Keflavk Airport
  • Day One is a grace day in Reykjavk, allowing guests to rest and acclimate themselves to Icelands time difference, purchase food for the trip, etc.

Day Two

  • Raufarhlshellir lava caves, near orlkshfn
  • Seljalandsfoss waterfall & surrounding area
  • rsmrk National Park
  • Stay at guesthouse in Vk

Day Three

  • Breathtaking Viking huts in the hills near Vk
  • Skgafoss waterfall & surrounding area
  • Slheimasandr & the spectacular wreckage of a crashed DC-3 aircraft Return to Vk for the night

Day Four

  • Slheimajkull glacier & surrounding area
  • Dyrhlaey & cliffs overlooking southern coastline
  • Hidden lava tunnels near Dyrhlaey
  • Reynisfjara
  • Return to Vk

Day Five

  • Various locations in the mountains above Vk & Mradalur
  • Skaftafell & Fellsjkull glaciers
  • Svartifoss (1hr walk from Skaftafell visitor centre)
  • Jkulsrln glacial lagoon & ice beach
  • Drive 1 hour back towards Vk, stay near Kirkjubjarklaustur

Day Six

  • Systrafoss in Kirkjubjarklaustur
  • Lakagigar (need 44)
  • Begin the drive back to Reykjavk, stay at guesthouse in Hella or Hvolsvllur.

Day Seven

  • Return to Reykjavk
  • Urban shoot around town
  • Meet bands/artists that night for practice or private show (dependent on artists availability at the time. Feel free to contact bodogger@comcast.net for more information.)

Bowen Staines has substantial experience in the fields of photography and filmmaking for over fifteen years, and has worked closely with National Geographic as a videographer and still photographer for the last six years. Bowen is also the owner of the Iceland-based film production company, Dont Panic Films (DontPanicIceland.com), his recent projects being music videos for Slstafirs Fjara, Sklmlds Gleipnir, and The Vintage Caravans Expand Your Mind. Bowen has directed videos for clients ranging from Dolly Parton and lafur Arnalds, to Alan Rickman and Leonard Cohen. Having worked on countless projects in Iceland, Bowen has extensive knowledge of the Icelandic countryside, the history of the island, and the intricacies of leading professional photography workshops.
